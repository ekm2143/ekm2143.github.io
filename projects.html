Description
Though it is uncertain whether students will return to campus in the Fall, it is necessary to rethink the way we used to do things before COVID-19. Social distancing will be the new normal, as will frequent online interactions between students and educators. We reimagine office hours to be structured around manageable queues and topic tags that help TAs and professors organize students, replacing the crowded help rooms of the past.

Neighborly

For Shoppers in the COVID-19 era

📍 Remote
🌟 March 2020 - April 2020
👩🏻‍💻 Solo Project

Learning Objectives
👉 User Research
👉 Market Research
👉 UX/UI Design (Sketch, Adobe XD)
👉 Staying Sane in Quarantine

Description
I began this project a couple weeks after Columbia students were cast out of campus to begin quarantine. Feeling helpless, and worrying about my parents who did all the grocery shopping for my family, I began to collect some informal user research to design a product that would make looking for high-demand items, like cleaning products, easier, while also encouraging social distancing. Neighborly includes a screen where users "register" for an appointment slot at a store, and report items that are low on stock while they're there (think Waze), saving their neighbors a wasted trip.
